>[!info] Core Concept: Covalent Bonds as Orbital Overlap
>A covalent bond is formed when atomic orbitals from two different atoms **overlap** in space, creating a region where a shared pair of electrons can exist.
>
>The extent of this overlap determines the bond's strength: `==greater overlap leads to a stronger, more stable bond==`.

---

## 🔬 The Two Types of Covalent Bonds: Sigma (σ) and Pi (π)

The geometry of how orbitals overlap results in two distinct types of covalent bonds.

### 1. Sigma (σ) Bonds

A sigma bond is formed by the **direct, head-on overlap** of atomic orbitals.

-   **Key Feature**: The electron density is concentrated and symmetrically distributed **directly between the two bonding nuclei**, along the internuclear axis.
-   **Formation**: Can be formed from the overlap of:
    -   `s-s` orbitals (as in H₂)
    -   `s-p` orbitals
    -   `p-p` orbitals (head-on)
    -   Hybridised orbitals (e.g., `sp³-s` or `sp²-sp²`)
-   **Strength**: Sigma bonds are **strong** due to the high degree of direct orbital overlap.

> [!tip] Rule of Thumb
> - All **single bonds** are sigma bonds.
> - The **first bond** in any multiple bond (double or triple) is always a sigma bond.

### 2. Pi (π) Bonds

A pi bond is formed by the **sideways overlap** of adjacent, parallel p-orbitals.

-   **Key Feature**: The electron density is concentrated in two regions, **above and below** the plane of the sigma bond. The internuclear axis itself has zero electron density (it's a nodal plane).
-   **Formation**: A π bond is *only* formed by the sideways overlap of **unhybridised p-orbitals**.
-   **Strength**: Pi bonds are **weaker** than sigma bonds because the sideways overlap is less effective (less direct) than head-on overlap.

> [!tip] Rule of Thumb
> - A **double bond** consists of `1 σ bond` and `1 π bond`.
> - A **triple bond** consists of `1 σ bond` and `2 π bonds` (oriented at 90° to each other).

## ⚖️ The Concept of Hybridisation

Hybridisation is a model used to explain how atoms (especially carbon) form stable bonds with specific geometries. It involves the **mixing of atomic orbitals** (like s and p) to create a new set of identical, degenerate **hybrid orbitals**. These new orbitals are perfectly shaped and oriented for forming strong sigma bonds.

## ✨ Application: Bonding in Syllabus Molecules

### Hydrogen ($\ce{H_2}$)
-   **Hybridisation**: None needed.
-   **Bonding**: The `1s` orbital of one H atom overlaps directly with the `1s` orbital of the other.
-   **Result**: One **σ bond**.

### Ethane ($\ce{C_2H_6}$)
-   **Hybridisation**: Each carbon atom is **sp³** hybridised.
-   **Bonding**:
    -   The C-C bond is a **σ bond** formed by the head-on overlap of two `sp³` hybrid orbitals.
    -   Each C-H bond is a **σ bond** formed by the overlap of an `sp³` orbital from carbon and a `1s` orbital from hydrogen.
-   **Result**: 7 σ bonds in total. The molecule has a tetrahedral geometry around each carbon.

### Ethene ($\ce{C_2H_4}$)
-   **Hybridisation**: Each carbon atom is **sp²** hybridised.
-   **Bonding**:
    -   The C=C double bond consists of:
        1.  One **σ bond** from the head-on overlap of two `sp²` hybrid orbitals.
        2.  One **π bond** from the sideways overlap of the unhybridised `p` orbital on each carbon.
    -   Each C-H bond is a **σ bond** (`sp²-s` overlap).
-   **Result**: 5 σ bonds and 1 π bond. The molecule is planar with 120° bond angles.

### Hydrogen Cyanide ($\ce{HCN}$)
-   **Hybridisation**: The central carbon atom is **sp** hybridised. (Nitrogen is also considered sp hybridised here).
-   **Bonding**:
    -   The C≡N triple bond consists of:
        1.  One **σ bond** from the head-on overlap of two `sp` hybrid orbitals (one from C, one from N).
        2.  **Two π bonds** from the sideways overlap of the two sets of unhybridised `p` orbitals on C and N.
    -   The H-C bond is a **σ bond** (`s-sp` overlap).
-   **Result**: 2 σ bonds and 2 π bonds. The molecule is linear with a 180° bond angle.

### Nitrogen ($\ce{N_2}$)
-   **Hybridisation**: Each nitrogen atom is **sp** hybridised.
-   **Bonding**:
    -   The N≡N triple bond consists of:
        1.  One **σ bond** from the `sp-sp` overlap.
        2.  **Two π bonds** from the sideways overlap of the remaining two `p` orbitals on each nitrogen atom.
-   **Result**: 1 σ bond and 2 π bonds.
