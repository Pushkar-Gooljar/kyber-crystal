## What is Enthalpy?
- The total chemical energy inside a substance/system is called the **enthalpy** (or heat content).
- When chemical reactions take place, changes in chemical energy take place and therefore the enthalpy changes.
- Enthalpy has units $kJmol^-1$, kilojoule per mole.

>[!info]  What is the System and Surroundings?
>##### System
>The system is the **molecules that are reacting** (i.e. the reaction itself)
>##### Surroundings
>The surroundings are **everything else** (e.g. the flask the reaction is taking place in, water or solution in calorimeter cup)

---
## Exothermic Reactions
- An exothermic reaction is one in which thermal energy is liberated from the system to the surroundings
- Causing an increase in temperature of the surroundings.
- Since [[#What is Enthalpy?|enthalpy]] decreases, the enthalpy change is negative, $\Delta H=-ve$

## Endothermic Reactions
- An endothermic reaction is one in which thermal energy is absorbed into the system from the surroundings
- Causing a decrease in temperature of the surroundings.
- Since [[#What is Enthalpy?|enthalpy]] increases, the enthalpy change is positive, $\Delta H=+ve$

# Reaction Pathway Diagrams
- A reaction pathway diagram shows the energies of the reactants, the transition state(s) and the products of the reaction with time.
- The *y-axis* shows the Enthalpy.
- The *x-axis* shows the progress of the reaction.
- Activation energy, $E_{A}$: minimum energy colliding particles must possess for a reaction to happen.
- Enthalpy change, $\Delta H$: the difference in enthalpy of products and reactants

> - The **transition state** is is a stage during the reaction at which chemical bonds are **partially broken and formed**.
> - The transition state is **very unstable** - it cannot be isolated and is higher in energy than the reactants and products

## Exothermic Reaction
- In an exothermic reaction, the reactants are higher in energy than the products.
- The reactants are therefore closer to in energy to the transition state (peak of diagram).
- This means that exothermic reactions have a lower activation energy compared to endothermic reactions.


---
## Endothermic Reaction
- In an endothermic reaction, the reactants are lower in energy than the products.
- The reactants are therefore further away in energy to the transition state (peak of diagram).
- The means that endothermic reactions have a higher activation energy compared to exothermic reactions.
