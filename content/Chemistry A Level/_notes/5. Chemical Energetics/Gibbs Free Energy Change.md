- Neither enthalpy $\mathrm{\Delta H}$ or entropy $\mathrm{\Delta S}$ alone can determine if a reaction will be feasible.
- Gibbs Free Energy (G) is a thermodynamic quantity that combines both factors to be the ultimate predictor of reaction feasibility.
- It represents the usable energy available in a system to do work.

**Gibbs Free Energy Equation:**
$$
\large
\begin{align}
\mathrm{\Delta G^{⦵} = \Delta H^{⦵} - T\Delta S^{⦵}}
\end{align}
$$
**Where:**
- $\mathrm{\Delta G^{⦵}}$ = Standard Gibbs free energy change
	- Unit: $\mathrm{kJmol^{-1}}$
- $\mathrm{\Delta H^{⦵}}$ = Standard enthalpy change
	- Unit: $\mathrm{kJmol^{-1}}$
- $\mathrm{T}$ = Thermodynamic temperature
	- Unit: Kelvin (K). (Kelvin = Celsius + 273 )
- $\mathrm{\Delta S^{⦵}}$ = Standard Entropy Change
	- Unit: $\mathrm{JK^{-1}mol^{-1}}$

>[!warning] Unit conversion is crucial
>Before using the Gibbs equation, you must convert the units of $\mathrm{\Delta S^{⦵}}$ from $\mathrm{JK^{-1}mol^{-1}}$ to $\mathrm{kJK^{-1}mol^{-1}}$ by dividing by 1000.


## Feasibility of Reactions
A reaction is feasible if $\mathrm{\Delta G^{⦵}}$ is negative



| $\mathrm{\Delta H^{⦵}}$ | $\mathrm{\Delta S^{⦵}}$ | $\mathrm{-T\Delta S^{⦵}}$ | $\mathrm{\Delta G^{⦵}}$                        | Feasible?                                 |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| -                       | +                       | -                         | negative                                       | Feasible at all temperatures              |
| +                       | +                       | -                         | becomes more negative as temperature increases | Feasible above a certain temperature <br> |
| -                       | -                       | +                         | becomes less negative as temperature increases | Feasible below a certain temperature      |
| +                       | -                       | +                         | positive                                       | never                                     |

## Calculating the temperature at which a reaction becomes feasible

- To find out at what temperature a reaction just becomes feasible ($\mathrm{\Delta G^{⦵}=0}$), we can rearrange the free energy equation:

When $\mathrm{\Delta G^{⦵}}=0$, $\mathrm{T\Delta S^{⦵}=\Delta H^{⦵}}$
Therefore, $\mathrm{T=\frac{\Delta H^{⦵}}{\Delta S^{⦵}}}$
