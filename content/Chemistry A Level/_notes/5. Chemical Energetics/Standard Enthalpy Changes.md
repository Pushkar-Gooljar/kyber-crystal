## Reaction
- Enthalpy change when the amount of reactants shown in the stoichiometric equation react to form products
- ...under standard conditions.
## Formation
Enthalpy change when one mol of a substance is formed from its constituent elements under standard conditions
## Combustion
enthalpy change when 1 mole of a compound is completely burnt in excess oxygen under standard conditions.

## Neutralisation
Enthalpy change when 1 mole of water is formed from an aqueous acid and an alkali under standard conditions

## Lattice
Enthalpy change when one mole of ionic solid forms from gaseous ions

## Solution
Enthalpy change when one mole of a compound is dissolved in excess water under standard conditions

## Hydration
enthalpy change when one mol of gaseous ions dissolves in excess water 
## Atomisation
enthalpy change when one mol of gaseous atoms is produced from its element in its standard state

## Electron affinity 
enthalpy change when one mol of electrons is gained by one mole of gaseous atoms to produce 1 mol of -1 ions

## Entropy
Number of possible arrangements of particles and energy in a system
