>[!info] Definition: Covalent Bonding
>- Covalent bond is the **electrostatic attraction** between the nuclei of two atoms and a shared pair of electrons.
>-  This sharing allows atoms, typically non-metals, to achieve a stable outer electron shell, usually a full octet, similar to a noble gas.

## Exceptions to the octet rule
- The octet rule states that atoms tend to form chemical bonds to achieve a stable electron configuration with eight electrons in their outer shell.
- However there are some notable exceptions:

### Less than 8 electrons 
- Example: Boron trifluoride ($\ce{BF_{3}}$).
- Boron has only 6 electrons in its outer shell

### More than 8 electrons
- Elements in period 3 and beyond can expand their octets.
- They use vacant $\mathrm{3d}$ orbitals to accommodate additional electrons.
