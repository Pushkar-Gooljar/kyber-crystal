## 1. Bond Energy (Bond Enthalpy)
>[!info] Definition: Bond Energy
>- Bond energy is the energy required to break one mole of a particular covalent bond in the gaseous state

## 2. Bond Length 
>[!info] Definition: Bond Length 
>- Bond length is the internuclear distance of two covalently bonded atoms


## 3. Using Bond Energy and Length to Compare Reactivity 
By considering both bond energies and bond lengths, we can compare the relative reactivity of different covalent molecules. Molecules with longer covalent bonds and lower bond energies, such as F2, tend to be more reactive than molecules with shorter bonds and higher bond energies, such as N2.
