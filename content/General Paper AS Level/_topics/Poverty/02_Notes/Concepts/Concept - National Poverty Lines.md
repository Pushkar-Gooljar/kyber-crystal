# Concept: National Poverty Lines

Unlike the global [[Concept - International Poverty Line (IPL)|IPL]], national poverty lines are set by individual countries and vary widely. They are often based on:
1.  The cost of a locally relevant "basket of goods" and services.
2.  A percentage of the median household income (common in developed nations).

See [[Example - USA National Poverty Line]] and [[Example - India National Poverty Line]] for contrast.
