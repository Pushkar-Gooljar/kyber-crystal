# Concept: Situational vs. Generational Poverty

**Situational Poverty:**
-   A temporary condition caused by a sudden crisis (job loss, medical emergency, natural disaster).

**Generational Poverty:**
-   A long-term pattern where poverty has persisted for at least two generations.
-   It is a systemic cycle reinforced by a lack of resources, networks, and skills.
-   Chronic stress and poor nutrition can lead to physiological and even epigenetic changes, making the cycle self-perpetuating.
