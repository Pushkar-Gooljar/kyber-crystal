The MPI is a measure of poverty that goes beyond income to capture deprivations across multiple dimensions. It was developed by OPHI and UNDP.

**Three Key Dimensions:**
1.  **Health:** (Nutrition, Child Mortality)
2.  **Education:** (Years of Schooling, School Attendance)
3.  **Living Standards:** (Cooking Fuel, Sanitation, Drinking Water, Electricity, Housing, Assets)

It helps identify *who* is poor and *how* they are poor.
