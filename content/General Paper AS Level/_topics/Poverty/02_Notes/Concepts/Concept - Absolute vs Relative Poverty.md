**Absolute Poverty:**
-   Severe deprivation of basic human needs (food, water, sanitation, health).
-   Standards are consistent across countries, often measured by the [[Concept - International Poverty Line (IPL)]].
-   Example: [[Example - Absolute Poverty in Chad]].

**Relative Poverty:**
-   Deprivation relative to the economic status of others in the *same society*.
-   Often defined as living below a certain percentage (e.g., 50-60%) of the median household income.
-   Example: [[Example - Relative Poverty in the UK]].
-   Oxfam notes that as absolute poverty falls, relative poverty and inequality are rising globally.
