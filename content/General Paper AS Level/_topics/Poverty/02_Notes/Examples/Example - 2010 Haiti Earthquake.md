The 2010 Haiti earthquake caused an estimated $7.8 billion in damage, equivalent to 120% of the country's 2009 GDP. The human toll was immense, with over 220,000 people killed and 1.5 million left homeless. The event highlights the disproportionate impact of natural disasters on the poor, who lack savings or insurance to cope with such shocks. For the poorest, the well-being loss from asset destruction is more than double the actual monetary value of the loss.
