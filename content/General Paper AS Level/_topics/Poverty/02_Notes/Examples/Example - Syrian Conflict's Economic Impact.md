Over a decade of conflict in Syria has caused the country's GDP to shrink to less than half its pre-conflict value. The poverty rate soared from 33% to a staggering 90%, and extreme poverty rose from a negligible level to 27%. The conflict has also destroyed critical infrastructure, including a third of health centres, and resulted in the loss of an estimated 618,000 lives, demonstrating the devastating cycle where conflict drives poverty and poverty fuels instability.
