Since 1978, China has lifted approximately 770 million people out of poverty, contributing to nearly three-quarters of the global reduction in extreme poverty. This was achieved through a dual strategy of broad-based economic transformation (market reforms) and a targeted "Precision Poverty Alleviation" policy. While a monumental success, challenges of inequality and regional disparity remain.
