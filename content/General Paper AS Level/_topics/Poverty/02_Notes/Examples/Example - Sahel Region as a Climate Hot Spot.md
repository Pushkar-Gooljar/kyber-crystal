The Sahel region in Africa is identified as a climate "hot spot." Here, a combination of severe droughts, desertification, and floods leads to a sharp decline in agricultural productivity and a loss of livelihoods for the agrarian societies that depend on the land. These climate-related shocks are a powerful driver of poverty and fuel mass migration as people are forced to leave their homes in search of food and water.
