Lack of access to quality education and healthcare creates a vicious cycle that traps individuals and families in poverty. It does this by limiting future opportunities (**human capital**) and draining current resources (**financial capital**).

---

### The Education Trap: Limiting Future Potential

Limited access to quality education prevents the development of the skills and knowledge needed for economic advancement. This directly causes poverty by:
- **Blocking Higher-Paying Jobs:** Without foundational literacy, numeracy, or specialized training, individuals are restricted to low-wage, unstable manual labour.
- **Perpetuating Intergenerational Poverty:** Children of parents with little education are statistically less likely to complete their own, locking the family into a cycle of low income.
    - See [[Statistic - High dropout rates for low-income students]].
- **Reducing Social Mobility:** Education is a primary pathway for individuals to improve their socioeconomic status beyond that of their parents.

### The Healthcare Trap: Draining Current Resources

Poor health and inaccessible medical care act as a direct drain on a family's financial resources and ability to earn an income. This is a primary driver of poverty through:

- **Catastrophic Health Costs:** A single serious illness or accident can bankrupt a family without health insurance or savings, forcing them into crippling debt or to sell critical assets (like land, tools, or livestock).
- **Loss of Income:** Illness prevents adults from working. When children are sick, parents must often sacrifice their own wages to provide care, reducing household income.
- **Reduced Productivity:** Chronic health issues, malnutrition, or disabilities decrease an individual's capacity to work effectively, leading to lower wages and job instability.

This creates a devastating feedback loop: poverty leads to worse health outcomes, and in turn, poor health locks people in poverty.
