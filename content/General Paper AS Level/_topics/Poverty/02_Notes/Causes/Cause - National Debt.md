A crippling national debt forces developing countries to allocate a significant portion of their national budget to debt service payments. This diverts critical funds away from essential social sectors like education, healthcare, and infrastructure, thereby perpetuating the poverty cycle by preventing investment in human capital and development. This is why debt relief, as seen in the [[Example - HIPC and MDRI Initiatives]], is a powerful anti-poverty tool.
