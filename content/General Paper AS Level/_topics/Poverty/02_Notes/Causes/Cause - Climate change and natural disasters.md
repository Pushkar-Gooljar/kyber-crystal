Environmental factors disproportionately harm the world's poorest populations.

-   **Climate Change:** Droughts, floods, and desertification reduce agricultural productivity and destroy livelihoods in agrarian societies. See [[Example - Sahel Region as Climate Hot Spot]].
-   **Natural Disasters:** The poor lack savings, insurance, and safety nets to cope with shocks. A disaster's well-being loss is amplified for the poorest. See [[Example - 2010 Haiti Earthquake]].
