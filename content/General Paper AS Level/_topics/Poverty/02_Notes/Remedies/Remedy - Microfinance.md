Microfinance provides small loans to impoverished individuals, particularly women, without requiring collateral. The model is founded on the principle that loans are more effective than charity in fostering self-employment and breaking the poverty cycle. The most famous example is the [[Example - Grameen Bank in Bangladesh]].
