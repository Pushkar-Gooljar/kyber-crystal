The assertion that democracy is the best form of government has been a cornerstone of Western political thought for centuries. Rooted in the Athenian principle of 'rule by the people', its modern form champions populist sovereignty, the protection of individual freedoms, and makes those in power accountable for their actions. However, in recent times, with the rise in political polarisation, and the perceived effectiveness of alternate systems like autocracies, this long held axiom has been facing more scrutiny.
